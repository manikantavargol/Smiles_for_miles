Belgaum Fort – The City of Diverse Existence

If you want to witness majesty at its best, pay a visit to Belgaum Fort. This is truly the epicentre of Belgaum tourism. This ancient monument represents the country’s spirit. When you are visiting the fort, you can see several Hindu shrines, Jain temples and mosques located inside the fort area. There are shrines of Goddess Durga and Ganpati at the entrance of the fort as well. Another of the prominent Belgaum attractions that you will get within the fort premises is the Safa Masjid. With its brilliant architecture and historical significance, Belgaum Fort should be on the top of your Belgaum travel guide.

Gokak Falls – In Harmony with Nature

The Gokak Falls holds a reputation for being one of the top places to visit in Belgaum and for all the right reasons. The Ghataprabha River gushes down from a height of 171 ft amidst lush greenery and beautiful mountains to form the stunning Gokak Falls. What’s bound to catch your attention is the hanging 200-m-long bridge. While you are around the Gokak Falls, you can visit the ancient Lord Mahalingeshwara temple and seek blessings. If you are a nature lover, this place will surely delight you

Ghataprabha Bird Sanctuary– Queer but Captivating

If you are near Gokak Falls, you can’t miss the Ghataprabha Bird Sanctuary, one of the most-visited among the Belgaum tourist places. This one is a must for bird-watchers and wildlife enthusiasts. It is home to several migratory birds, including the European White Stork and the Demoiselle Crane. Covering an area of 29 sq km, the sanctuary is enclosed by the Ghataprabha River. So, you know, you will get amazing views. November to March is the best time to go to Belgaum if you wish to stop by the Ghataprabha Bird Sanctuary.

Savadatti Yellamma Temple

Travel 70 km from Belgaum and you will reach Yellamma Temple. It is a famed pilgrimage site in the region. Situated atop a hill, the temple is dedicated to Goddess Renuka. This place will satisfy both your spiritual and natural senses. Other than Goddess Renuka’s temple, there are temples of Lord Ganesha, Lord Parashurama, and Lord Kalabhairava as the presiding deities. Thousands of devotees from different parts of the country visit this iconic temple every year. During the Yellamma Jatra festival, these numbers increase nearly two-fold. Needless to say, your Belgaum sightseeing must include the Yellamma Temple.

Godachinmalki Falls or Markandeya Falls – City of Old World Charm

Belgaum is filled with waterfalls and another addition to the list of enchanting falls in the region is the Godachinmalki Falls. There are many reasons for this fall to be among the famous Belgaum tourist spots. Located in the Markandeya River, the beauty of the falls is truly unmatched. The river first cascades down from an elevation of 25 m and falls for the second time from a height of about 20 m, creating a visual treat. What’s more? You will get to trek a short distance to witness the allure of this majestic waterfall. If you are spending a little time here, you can visit the Hidkal Dam and Shirur Dam situated nearby.

Parasgad Fort – Nature’s Fury and Peace

Parasgad Fort is not only among the noted Belgaum places to visit, but also one of the oldest forts in the region. The 400-years old fort still stands with all its might, attracting tourists from far and wide. On the top of this fort, you can find a small shrine dedicated to Lord Hanuman. There are statues of Lord Rama, Goddess Sita, Lord Parashurama, and a Shiva linga as well. If you wish to spend some time away from the city crowd and get lost in spiritually, visiting Parasgad Fort can be a great choice.

Jamboti Hills and Falls – Tranquility at its Best

Your trip will remain incomplete if you don’t include Jamboti Hills and Falls in your list of the finest tourist places near Belgaum. Jamboti Hills is renowned as the birthplace of the Mandovi River - Goa’s longest river. The surreal environment, cool breeze, and serene atmosphere make it a must-visit place. Whether you are a nature lover, ardent photographer, trekker, or peace seeker, a visit to Jamboti Hills will give you an unforgettable experience.

Military Mahadeva Temple – The Majestic Temple Towns

With Lord Shiva as the presiding deity, Military Mahadeva Temple ranks highly on the list of the popular tourist places to see in Belgaum. It is one of the oldest temples in the region and is wonderfully managed by the Indian Army of Southern Command. The temple is known for having an alluring black Shivalinga. The spiritual ambience, religious aura, and peaceful environment will leave you spellbound. There is also a well-manicured garden and a small zoo within the temple premises.

Rajhansgad Yellur Fort–Taste of Authentic Karnataka

Rajhansgad Yellur Fort is one of the most impressive historical places to visit Belgaum, Karnataka. Archaeological sources reveal that this fort has been ruled by many royal clans of India; namely Marathas, Peshwas, Hoysalas, and Bahamanis. Perched high on a hill, one can enjoy trekking up to the fort and admire the spectacular views of the lush green surrounding. Moreover, there is a sweet water well and an ancient Shiva temple, located at the hilltop.

Kamala Basti–Reach the Zenith of Beauty

Kamala Basti is one of the best places to visit in Belgaum. Located inside the Belgavi Fort complex, this religious place is built in Chalukyan style architecture and houses a black stone idol of Neminatha. With intricate artwork and sculptures, Kamala Basti is a perfect combination of religious significance and architectural beauty and the Mukhamantapa which is a lotus curved on the ceiling is surely a stunner.

Kittur Fort & Palace – A Fortified Marvel

Built by Rani Chennamma, Kittur Fort and Palace is one of the most interesting places near Belgaum. Apart from temples of Maruti, Kalmeshwara, Dyamavva and Nathapanthi matha, this place also houses a marvelous archaeological museum inside the palace. With an impressive collection of weapons, armors, knick-knacks, jewellery, costumes and historical sculptures and inscriptions, this museum is a visual treat for archeology enthusiasts.

Kote Kere – Bask in Royalty

If you are holidaying in Belgaum with your family and kids, there is no way you can skip Kote Kore. This is a well-maintained lake in the city, surrounded by luxuriant forests. With speedboats, pedal boats and toy train rides, this place is perfect for day excursions, picnics, fun, and frolic.
